Overview

Sound Transit is building a new garage at Sumner Station, so that more people can conveniently access regional transit and enjoy traffic-free travel.

Construction begins later this year, with an expected opening in early 2026. Keep scrolling for garage designs and what to expect during construction.

Some improvements for pedestrians and bicyclists are finished, including sidewalk and lighting enhancements in the station area and a new sidewalk and multi-use path on the Traffic Avenue/ SR 410 interchange. Additional improvements will be built with the garage, including a new pedestrian plaza adjacent to the platform area.

The new parking garage, located on the site of Sumner Station‚Äôs current surface parking, will have 4.5 levels and approximately 600 stalls. The garage will border Harrison Street on the south, extending from the station platform on the west to neighboring properties on the east, spanning over Station Lane. There will be surface parking on Harrison Street and north of the garage.

A new plaza will be added between Station Lane and Academy Street. The plaza will be accentuated with a paving pattern and tree mounds to create pathways connecting the garage to the platform.

Garage access

  • Vehicles will have three entrances to the garage: one from Academy St. to the north, one from Harrison St. to the south, and one from Station Lane to the south.
  • All vehicles will exit the garage at the Harrison Street or Station Lane exits. After exiting, all vehicles will be directed south on Station Lane.
  • The primary pedestrian pathway from the garage to the platform will be from the stair tower at the northwest corner of the garage.

Bus routing

After the garage is open, Sound Transit Express buses 578 and 596 will enter and exit the station via Station Lane to the south.

Station Lane and Thompson Street intersection

To accommodate buses turning into and out of Station Lane at Thompson Street, we will be widening the northwest corner of the intersection and moving the crosswalk from the southwest corner to the southeast corner of the intersection.

Walking, rolling and bicycling

Sound Transit has contributed $7.5 million to the City of Sumner to make walking, rolling, and bicycling to the station and in the area easier and safer. This work, which is already complete, includes sidewalk ramps and lighting enhancements in the station area and a new sidewalk and multi-use path on the Traffic Avenue/ SR 410 interchange. In addition, the City of Sumner recently completed new bicycle lanes on Academy Street, helping more local residents get to the station by bicycle.

Bike parking

We will be replacing the bicycle lockers at Sumner Station with new on-demand BikeLink lockers, which can be rented for $0.05 per hour. By having a more flexible locker arrangement, we expect more people can conveniently ride their bicycles to the station and store them securely. The new lockers will be located on the north end of the platform.

There will also be bicycle racks located on the ground floor of the parking garage.

You shared your perspectives, and we listened

In 2017 and 2019, Sumner community members shared input on the visual design of the garage. Preferences included:

  • Matching the small-town feel of Sumner and fitting in with Sumner‚Äôs Main Street.
  • Not standing out or incorporating bright, bold colors.
  • Incorporating brick/masonry materials, a natural color palette and pedestrian friendly details.
  • Utilize changes in materials, textures and colors to bring scale and detail to each fa√ßade.

We responded with the following design features:

  • A color palette inspired by the surrounding environment.
  • Incorporating brick elements and textured concrete.
  • Weather-protected spaces for pedestrians.
  • Decorative metal screening.

As transit and passengers speed past the parking structure, the angled fins (screening) create a sense of movement that echoes the patterns of native riparian grasses.

The fa√ßade allows natural light to filter through at varying degrees, enlivening the interior spaces throughout, and delighting the eye of travelers within the structure, as well as those passing by at the exterior.

At the ground level, a mix of materials, including metal screening, perforated box-rib metal panels and brick, along with landscaping, evokes a design language from Sumner‚Äôs historic downtown. Generous canopies and a seating area recessed into the building provide refuge from inclement weather.

The open design pattern sheltering the parking structure is inspired by natural elements found in the forests of the Pacific Northwest. The unique screening creates dynamic interchanges of light and shadow, acting as both windbreak and sunshade while still allowing natural ventilation.

The intermittent horizontal bands emphasize the activity and movement around the structure, while providing visual breaks in the overall building height. Textured board-formed concrete walls modulate the building‚Äôs length with changes in material and height.

The south entrance portal is marked by signage integrated into the canopy, creating a dedicated entry point for buses to safely pass through. Brick wraps around the building continuing to line the pedestrian path along Station Lane.

The weather-protected stair tower will feature art in the stair glazing on its north fa√ßade and allow light to pass through brick patterned glass elements on the west side. Glass on the east provides views into the two elevators provided.

Construction

Construction impacts

Passenger parking - 

Parking for transit riders will be significantly reduced for approximately two years, to make room for garage construction. At least 110 stalls will always be available, including the 68 stalls in the lot north of Maple Street on the western side of the railroad tracks.

There are several other options for getting to a Sounder Station during construction:

  • Drive to Puyallup Station (just 3 miles from Sumner Station) and park in the new garage or surface lot, which added 673 new stalls to the station.
  • Park at Bonney Lake Park-and-Ride Lot (5 miles from Sumner Station with 356 stalls) and catch ST Express 596 to Sumner Station. (The 596 does not serve every train - please review the schedule.)
  • Walk to Sumner Station.
  • Ride your bicycle to the station.
    • o New on-demand BikeLink lockers will be available at Sumner Station starting later this year. Lock up your bike for the day, then pick it up after your trip.
    • o You can also take your bike with you on the train. Learn more about taking your bike on Sounder.
  • Carpool to the station. Carpools will get access to reserved areas in the parking lot north of Maple Street. Sign up here for more information and to be notified when permits start.
    • Carpool parking permits are available free of charge to groups of two or more transit riders, who regularly drive together to catch the train. Permits give you access to reserved parking areas when arriving with two or more transit riders in a vehicle during morning rush hour. The reserved area will be in the lot north of Maple Street.

Phase 1 Construction layout + 

During Phase 1, we will close most parking south of Maple Street for a few months. Two areas will remain open for parking:

  • The southwest corner of the station, which will include ADA stalls for passengers with disabilities, as well as 15-minute drop-off/pick up stalls.
  • The lot north of Maple Street, which will include spaces reserved for carpool permit holders.

Buses will enter the station from Maple St., exiting on to Harrison St. Bus stop locations may change, and we will notify riders in advance.

Phase 2 Construction layout + 

During Phase 2, which is expected to last up to two years, stalls south of Academy Street will be closed. Two areas will be open for parking:

  • The Narrow Street area, which will include ADA stalls for passengers with disabilities, as well as 15-minute drop-off/pick up stalls.
  • The lot north of Maple Street, which will include spaces reserved for carpool permit holders.

Bus routing will change, and bus stop locations may change. We will notify riders in advance and provide updated maps as we approach this phase of construction.

Neighborhood impacts

Streets and sidewalks

Partial closures or temporary sidewalk closures could take place on nearby streets. We will preserve access to all homes and businesses. We will send notice to residents and businesses in advance.

Noise

Work hours are generally 6 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday. Moderate noise and vibration can be expected between 7 a.m. and 4:30 p.m. If any night work is needed, that will be approved by the City and we will notify neighbors well in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Garage design

Will the garage have bright lights that affect neighbors? + 

ST follows industry standards for safe lighting and the state energy codes. The garage and site lighting strives to promote safety while minimizing intrusive lighting to the neighborhood. Lighting is designed to focus the light source within ST property lines and to avoid spilling beyond. The design team is currently working on choosing proper equipment and considering how to carefully mount lights to reduce glare and contain light to the site.

Parking policies

Will I have to pay to park at the garage? + 

Sound Transit is considering charging parking fees in the future at some or all park-and-ride facilities. An online open house is available until Oct. 22 at soundtransitfaresandparking.infocommunity.org.

Safety and security

What safety and security features are included in this garage?     + 

  • Landscaping designed to ensure clear visibility of vehicles and pedestrians.
  • Ample lighting in the garage interior and exterior.
  • Visible stairwells and elevator shafts.
  • Security cameras throughout the garage, including elevators.
  • Emergency phones are on each floor, at every corner.
  • Random security and police patrols 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Sound Transit also has a security operations center that monitors activity across our facilities 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Traffic and parking effects

How will the garage affect area traffic? + 

All vehicles will leave the garage via Station Lane on the south end of the site, then turn right on Thompson Street. Buses will enter and leave the station from the south on Station Lane.

Can I park on a neighborhood street near the station when I ride Sounder? + 

The City of Sumner zoned most streets near the station as "RPZ-3", which means only residents with a residential parking pass are allowed to park for more than three hours. When parking is reduced during construction, we recommend all passengers use alternate options, such as parking at Bonney Lake Park-and-ride, parking at Puyallup Station, applying for an HOV parking permit, or walking or bicycling to the station.

Sounder trains

Why doesn‚Äôt Sounder run more often? + 

Sounder runs primarily on tracks owned by the BNSF Railroad, used heavily for freight movements. In addition to regular weekday service, we provide special event trains for select events, like some Seahawks, Sounders FC, and Mariners games. See the event service schedule. As well, we are updating the Sounder South Strategic Plan this year and considering whether to prioritize adding new trips rather than longer trains. New trips, which would depend on agreement with BNSF, could be on weekends, or weekday mid-day or evenings. Take our survey by Oct. 29!

How do I know which track the train is arriving on? + 

Trains traveling northbound in the morning and southbound in the afternoon (peak direction) generally use the primary platform on the east side of the tracks, near most of the parking and the bus loop. Morning southbound trains and afternoon northbound trains (reverse peak direction) generally use the platform on the north side of the tracks. A station agent is on site when trains are running and can direct passengers to the right track or notify them if there are last-minute track changes.

Background: Sounder South and Regional Bus Service

Sounder S Line trains offer dependable, traffic-free travel between Lakewood and Seattle. We run 13 round trips on weekdays, and it takes a little over 40 minutes to travel from Sumner to Seattle. For details, read our schedule. We also run trains for select special events like Seahawks, Sounders FC and Mariners games. Adult fares range from $3.75 to $5.75 one way, fares for seniors and low-income individuals are lower, and all youth 18 and under ride free.

Powered by clean-burning, ultra-low sulfur diesel, Sounder runs primarily on tracks owned by BNSF Railway. While Sound Transit owns the trains and stations and provides security, BNSF operates Sounder and Amtrak provides maintenance.

Sounder trains offer comfortable seats, surge-protected outlets for laptops and tablets, Wi-Fi service, storage for up to four bicycles per car, overhead storage, climate control and restrooms. Some cars have worktables.

Commuter Connector

Do you work in Sumner‚Äôs industrial area? A new shuttle service may be for you! The City of Sumner launched the Commuter Connector in early 2023 to give workers a direct connection from Sumner Station. The Connector meets Sounder trains and runs every 20 minutes, when Sounder is running. It then drops employees off at their worksite and brings them back to the station again when it is time to return home. Learn more about the Commuter Connector!

ST Express 578

This Sound Transit bus connects Sumner with Puyallup, Auburn, Federal Way, and downtown Seattle and runs when Sounder doesn't. A trip from Sumner to downtown Seattle takes a little over an hour. See the 578 schedule.
